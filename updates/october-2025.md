# October 2025

Last month I figured out how to put stacks of interactive SVG cards into browsers, and once I got that working I asked myself where else I could put them.
I took a look at the list of extensions in Xcode, and started to look for any that could be interesting with my magistacks. 
One caught my eye...

At the end of the day, you can think of a magistack as a decision tree where all the branches are represented by keys that are strings of some length.
Stephen Cole Kleene, professor of Mathematics at UW Madison, had a theorem, no doubt discovered over some pints of New Glarus, that all languages of deterministic finite automata were the same.
In 1999, Shigetaka Kurita used Kleene's insight to create a character set known to the world as _emoji_. 

Last week I used Kurita's invention to encode decision tree keys to display hypercard-like interactive SVGs in a keyboard. 

That's a lot of words to say, I made it so if you put ☮️💚🏴‍☠️ somewhere, and then use a keyboard it can do something.
Don't worry about the something it's going to do just yet.
The key, is: where are there keyboards?
